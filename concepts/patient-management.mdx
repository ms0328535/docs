---
title: "Patient Management: Add, Search, and Remove Records"
sidebarTitle: "Patients"
description: "How the Hospital Management System stores and manages patient records using a singly linked list, with auto-assigned IDs and prepend insertion."
---

The Hospital Management System stores every patient as a node in a singly linked list. You never assign an ID yourself — the system automatically issues the next available integer starting at **1** and increments it with every new patient. This keeps patient records unique and easy to reference when you queue an appointment request.

## Adding a Patient

Select **option 1** from the main menu. The system prompts you for a name of up to **47 characters**. After you enter the name, the system assigns the next sequential ID, prepends the new node to the head of the patient list, and confirms the addition.

```text
=== Hospital Simple Menu ===
...
Choose an option: 1
Enter patient name: Alice Nguyen
Added patient 1 : Alice Nguyen
```

Adding a second patient continues the counter:

```text
Choose an option: 1
Enter patient name: Bob Martinez
Added patient 2 : Bob Martinez
```

## Viewing Patients

Select **option 3** to display all current patients. Because new patients are prepended to the head of the linked list, the list always appears in **most-recently-added order** — the newest patient appears first.

```text
Choose an option: 3
Patients (id : name):
  2 : Bob Martinez
  1 : Alice Nguyen
```

If no patients have been added yet, the system prints:

```text
(no patients)
```

## Deleting a Patient

Select **option 2** to remove a patient by ID. The system traverses the linked list, unlinks the matching node, and frees its memory.

```text
Choose an option: 2
Enter patient id to delete: 1
Deleted patient 1
```

If you supply an ID that does not exist, the system prints an error and leaves the list unchanged:

```text
Choose an option: 2
Enter patient id to delete: 99
Patient 99 not found
```

<Warning>
Deleting a patient does **not** remove their scheduled appointments from the schedule list. Any confirmed appointments that referenced that patient remain in the schedule but display **`[deleted]`** in place of the patient's name when you view scheduled appointments (option 10).
</Warning>

## Patient IDs

Patient IDs are **never reused**. The ID counter only ever increments — even after a deletion the freed ID is gone permanently. For example, if you add patients 1 and 2, delete patient 1, and then add a new patient, the new patient receives ID 3, not 1.

Before referencing a patient in an appointment request, use **option 3** to list all current patients and confirm the correct ID. Writing down the ID printed immediately after adding a patient is the most reliable approach.
