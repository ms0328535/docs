The Hospital Management System maintains a LIFO (last-in, first-out) undo stack alongside the appointment schedule. Whenever you need to reverse a scheduling decision, choose option **9** from the menu. The system deactivates the most recently created active appointment — without deleting any data — so you always have a full audit trail of what happened during the session.

## How Undo Works

The undo mechanism is built on a fixed-capacity integer stack that tracks scheduled appointment IDs in the order they were created.

- **Each time option 8 processes a request successfully**, the new appointment's ID is pushed onto the undo stack. This happens automatically; you do not need to do anything extra.
- **Each time you choose option 9**, the system pops the top ID from the stack and looks up the corresponding appointment in the schedule array. It then sets that appointment's `active` flag to `0`, marking it as **undone**.
- **Undone appointments remain visible** when you list the schedule with option 10. They appear alongside active appointments with the status label `undone` rather than `active`. This preserves a complete session history.
- **The appointment's slot in the schedule array is never physically removed.** The record stays in place; only its status changes. This means the total count of stored appointments (active + undone) continues to grow toward the 100-appointment session limit.

## Using Undo

The following example shows what the schedule looks like before and after calling option 9.

**Step 1 — Schedule an appointment (option 8):**

```text
=== Hospital Simple Menu ===
Choose an option: 8
Scheduled appointment s4: patient Bob Tanaka (p2) -> doctor Dr. Priya Nair (d3) at 2025-08-12 14:00 (from req #5)
```

**Step 2 — View the schedule before undo (option 10):**

```text
=== Hospital Simple Menu ===
Choose an option: 10
Scheduled appointments:
  s1: Alice Fernandez (p1) -> Dr. Marcus Obi (d2) at 2025-08-10 09:00 [active]
  s2: Carol Singh (p3) -> Dr. Sarah Chen (d1) at 2025-08-10 11:30 [active]
  s3: Bob Tanaka (p2) -> Dr. Sarah Chen (d1) at 2025-08-11 10:00 [active]
  s4: Bob Tanaka (p2) -> Dr. Priya Nair (d3) at 2025-08-12 14:00 [active]
```

**Step 3 — Undo the last appointment (option 9):**

```text
=== Hospital Simple Menu ===
Choose an option: 9
Undid appointment s4 (patient Bob Tanaka p2 doctor Dr. Priya Nair d3 at 2025-08-12 14:00)
```

**Step 4 — View the schedule after undo (option 10):**

```text
=== Hospital Simple Menu ===
Choose an option: 10
Scheduled appointments:
  s1: Alice Fernandez (p1) -> Dr. Marcus Obi (d2) at 2025-08-10 09:00 [active]
  s2: Carol Singh (p3) -> Dr. Sarah Chen (d1) at 2025-08-10 11:30 [active]
  s3: Bob Tanaka (p2) -> Dr. Sarah Chen (d1) at 2025-08-11 10:00 [active]
  s4: Bob Tanaka (p2) -> Dr. Priya Nair (d3) at 2025-08-12 14:00 [undone]
```

Appointment **s4** is now `undone`. The other three appointments are unaffected. Call option **9** again to undo **s3**, then **s2**, and so on — each call works one step further back.

## Limits

- **The undo stack holds up to 50 entries** (`STCAP = 50`). If you schedule 50 appointments without calling undo once, the stack is full and you will see `undo stack full` on any further scheduling attempt. In practice, a mix of scheduling and undoing keeps the stack well within capacity.
- **Undo always targets the most recently scheduled active appointment.** You cannot skip over a recent entry to undo an older one. The stack is strictly LIFO.
- **If the undo stack is empty** (no appointments have been scheduled, or all pushed IDs have already been popped), option 9 prints:
  ```text
  Nothing to undo
  ```
- **If the same appointment ID is somehow popped twice** (for example, if the internal state is manipulated externally), the system detects that the appointment is already inactive and prints:
  ```text
  Appointment s<id> already undone
  ```

<Warning>
  Undo does **not** restore the original appointment request to the queue. Once a request has been processed with option 8, it is permanently removed from the queue regardless of whether you later undo the resulting appointment. If you need to re-schedule an undone appointment, you must re-submit the request manually using option 6 and process it again with option 8.
</Warning>
