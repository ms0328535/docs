This walkthrough guides you through a realistic Hospital Management System session from start to finish. You will register doctors and patients, submit appointment requests, confirm them through the processing queue, review the schedule, and correct a mistake with the undo feature. Follow each step in order and use the terminal interactions as a reference for what to type and what to expect.

<Steps>

  <Step title="Register Doctors (Option 4)">
    Start by adding the doctors who will be receiving appointments. Choose option **4** at the menu for each doctor.

    ```text
    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 4
    Enter doctor name: Dr. Sarah Chen
    Added doctor 1 : Dr. Sarah Chen

    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 4
    Enter doctor name: Dr. Marcus Obi
    Added doctor 2 : Dr. Marcus Obi

    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 4
    Enter doctor name: Dr. Priya Nair
    Added doctor 3 : Dr. Priya Nair
    ```

    You now have doctors with IDs **1**, **2**, and **3**. Keep these IDs handy — you will need them when you queue appointment requests.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Register Patients (Option 1)">
    Add the patients who need appointments. Choose option **1** for each patient.

    ```text
    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 1
    Enter patient name: Alice Fernandez
    Added patient 1 : Alice Fernandez

    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 1
    Enter patient name: Bob Tanaka
    Added patient 2 : Bob Tanaka

    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 1
    Enter patient name: Carol Singh
    Added patient 3 : Carol Singh
    ```

    Patients are assigned IDs **1**, **2**, and **3** in the order you added them.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify Registrations (Options 3 and 5)">
    Confirm that all records were saved correctly before proceeding. Use option **3** to list patients and option **5** to list doctors.

    ```text
    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 3
    Patients (id : name):
      3 : Carol Singh
      2 : Bob Tanaka
      1 : Alice Fernandez

    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 5
    Doctors (id : name):
      1 : Dr. Sarah Chen
      2 : Dr. Marcus Obi
      3 : Dr. Priya Nair
    ```

    Patients appear most recently added first. Doctors appear sorted in ascending ID order.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Queue Appointment Requests (Option 6)">
    Submit appointment requests for the patients you just registered. Choose option **6** and provide a patient ID, doctor ID, and a free-form date/time string for each request.

    ```text
    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 6
    Enter patient id: 1
    Enter doctor id: 2
    Enter date/time (string): 2025-08-10 09:00
    Enqueued request #1  patient 1 -> doctor 2  at 2025-08-10 09:00

    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 6
    Enter patient id: 3
    Enter doctor id: 1
    Enter date/time (string): 2025-08-10 11:30
    Enqueued request #2  patient 3 -> doctor 1  at 2025-08-10 11:30
    ```

    You now have two pending requests in the queue.
  </Step>

  <Step title="View the Pending Queue (Option 7)">
    Before processing, use option **7** to confirm what is waiting in the queue and in what order it will be handled.

    ```text
    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 7
    Request queue :
      #1  p1 -> d2  @ 2025-08-10 09:00
      #2  p3 -> d1  @ 2025-08-10 11:30
    ```

    Requests are listed oldest first. Request **#1** will be processed first.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Process Each Request (Option 8)">
    Convert each pending request into a confirmed scheduled appointment by choosing option **8**. The system validates both IDs and creates the appointment.

    ```text
    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 8
    Scheduled appointment s1: patient Alice Fernandez (p1) -> doctor Dr. Marcus Obi (d2) at 2025-08-10 09:00 (from req #1)

    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 8
    Scheduled appointment s2: patient Carol Singh (p3) -> doctor Dr. Sarah Chen (d1) at 2025-08-10 11:30 (from req #2)
    ```

    Both requests are now fully scheduled. The queue is empty.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the Confirmed Schedule (Option 10)">
    Use option **10** to see all scheduled appointments, their status, and the patient and doctor details.

    ```text
    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 10
    Scheduled appointments:
      s1: Alice Fernandez (p1) -> Dr. Marcus Obi (d2) at 2025-08-10 09:00 [active]
      s2: Carol Singh (p3) -> Dr. Sarah Chen (d1) at 2025-08-10 11:30 [active]
    ```

    Both appointments show status `active`. The session now has a clean, confirmed schedule.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Undo if Needed (Option 9)">
    Suppose you scheduled the second appointment by mistake. Use option **9** to reverse it. Undo always targets the most recently scheduled appointment.

    ```text
    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 9
    Undid appointment s2 (patient Carol Singh p3 doctor Dr. Sarah Chen d1 at 2025-08-10 11:30)
    ```

    Check option **10** again to confirm the status change:

    ```text
    === Hospital Simple Menu ===
    Choose an option: 10
    Scheduled appointments:
      s1: Alice Fernandez (p1) -> Dr. Marcus Obi (d2) at 2025-08-10 09:00 [active]
      s2: Carol Singh (p3) -> Dr. Sarah Chen (d1) at 2025-08-10 11:30 [undone]
    ```

    Appointment **s2** is now marked `undone`. Appointment **s1** remains `active`. You can re-queue the request for Carol Singh using option **6** and process it again when ready.
  </Step>

</Steps>

## Tips for Managing a Busy Queue

- **Process regularly to avoid queue overflow.** The queue holds a maximum of **8 pending requests**. If you try to enqueue a ninth, you will see `Request queue is full`. Choose option **8** to process existing requests and free up slots before submitting more.
- **Check queue depth before submitting.** Use option **7** to see how many requests are pending. If you are close to the 8-request limit, process some before adding more.

<Tip>
  Keep a written note of your current doctor and patient IDs. Because IDs are assigned sequentially and never reused after deletions, a quick reference list lets you enter them accurately and quickly — especially during a busy session with many records.
</Tip>
